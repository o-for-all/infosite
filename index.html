How the online-marketplace works

The O-Marketplace makes sustainable consumption simpler and more easily affordable by using profits of its retail market in two ways

  • Profits from the sales of sustainable products are returned to buyers as credit.
    • Or
  • Profits from the sale of non-sustainable products are donated to projects that offset the negative impact of our consumption.

A sustainability-rating for each product is used as the basis for the profit distribution. Every verified person can contribute to this rating by giving their own rating or by supporting someone else's. Everyone has to provide background information on which they base their rating on and share his*her*their insights into the product, its production and its vendors.

O participates and strengthens everyone's ratings by providing, expanding, and maintaining an open sustainability encyclopedia, video profiles of manufacturers and vendors, and an ongoing exchange with all marketplace participants.

Why it works like that

We have no time to wait for people to slowly transition to a more sustainable lifestyle out of pure insight and understanding.

There are four major hindrances preventing sustainable consumption and production on scale:

  1. Not all products can be truly sustainable but are viewed as a necessity of modern life.
  2. For most people (82%) sustainable consumption appears to be too expensive.
  3. More than half regard sustainable consumption to be a hassle and too time consuming to do right (greenwashing).
  4. Producers and vendors have little incentive to be transparent beyond basic information.

All of these hindrances are lifted by O-Marketplace by

  • making every purchase through O-Marketplace more sustainable due to its commitment to support sustainable actions.
  • making sustainability more visible and at the same time more nuanced and individual than just a generic label.
  • making sustainable consumption rewarding also on a monetary level.

This way the roadblocks are gone and people can more quickly adopt a more sustainable lifestyle and, most importantly, learn, share and grow together - for the benefit of all.

A customer story based on almost true events

‘My first experience with O? Well I needed to buy a new laptop a few months ago, after my old one just fell apart. While I was researching and looking for reviews I saw O with like “MacBook Pro X - the most sustainable powerhouse” as headline. I remembered a friend of mine told me about O and that it’s a sustainable marketplace or something. But I very much doubted that the MacBook Pro can be sustainable in any way. So of course I went on the O-Marketplace and I saw all this in depth information about how Apple asks you to send in your old Apple products so they can recycle them or dispose of them appropriately and what not. I mean it was way better explained by the other people there. Anyway I didn’t buy the laptop, because I need Windows on my machine. But I saw an attached article comparing all the latest laptops similar to the MacBook Pro X. Turns out Dell basically does the same thing as Apple and was only 3 points less sustainable… from 100 points. So from there it was quite easy to choose the manufacturer and the article also pointed out which model is best for which use case. Since I only use my laptop to write and watch Netflix I didn’t actually need a powerhouse. But the best part was, one month after I bought my laptop I received a message that I got 51 Euros as credit. Turns out because the laptop was 70% sustainable and was like 980 Euro I got part of the profits back for buying more sustainably. I mean O is amazing. There is so much more to it, but it's best to just discover it yourself. It opened up a new world for me.’
